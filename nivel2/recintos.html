Bogota Campus


The growth of the digital economy in Colombia has a great demand for talent to which the country cannot respond. According to Fedesoft and MinTIC statistics, it is estimated that in Colombia the deficit of software development professionals is more than 50 thousand people. However, the need for talent focuses not only on technical skills (since the future of work is the automation of repetitive tasks), but also focuses on the development of soft skills such as critical thinking, work on team and assertive communication. Bogotá is not the first city that comes to mind when you think about entrepreneurship, technology and innovation, but in Latin America, things are starting to grow rapidly. With strong government support and tax exemptions for foreign investors, Bogotá's position as a hugely influential city in South America is accelerating. Our Bogotá campus is located in the district of zone G, close to important companies and the development of the technological sector. The city is home to the country's first unicorn, Rappi, which is also one of the main donors of the program's operation. With the high cost of education in Colombia and the few job opportunities in traditional careers, Holberton arrives with an innovative and accessible proposal for people who want to learn to program. In addition, the school focuses on providing essential social skills training for any worker.




Medellin Campus


Also known as the City of Eternal Spring, Medellín has established itself as a pioneer in technology and innovation. Named the most innovative city in the world in 2013, it has also become the Center of the World Economic Forum for the Fourth Industrial Revolution for Latin America. The new global companies have made Medellin their home, including Ayenda, backed by Softbank, the autonomous delivery company KiwiCampus and the builder of artificial intelligence companies AI Fund. Large global consulting firms such as Huge Inc, Accenture, Endava and Globant are also taking advantage of Medellin's talent. Our campus is located in the gastronomic and cultural center of El Poblado, 5 minutes walk from the metro station and within walking distance of restaurants, bars and discos.



Cali Campus

A city of 3.4 million people, Cali is the third largest city in Colombia and the most important city on the southwest coast of Colombia. An economic powerhouse, Cali is home to some of Colombia's largest and most influential companies such as Carvajal, Open Software, Delima, Datecsa, Eficacia, Colombina, Tecnoquimicas and Lafrancol. Cali also hosts many multinational companies such as Colgate, Baxter, Johnson & Johnson and more. As a cultural destination, it is known as the "Salsa Capital of the World" due to its famous salsa and nightlife festivals. A growing technological city, local and international companies recognize the talent and momentum of the local workforce. In combination with Cali Zonamerica, a special economic Free Zone in Colombia that supports a global services market, Cali is on its way to becoming one of the world's leading technological destinations. Our campus in Cali is located within Cali Zonamerica. As our students learn, they will be surrounded by opportunities to take their software engineering education at the Silicon Valley level directly.
